Interviewer Eligibility

To participate as an ASC interviewer, you must confirm:

  • You don't work in the college admission process
  • Your child isn't applying this year
  • You don't interview undergrads for another school

Getting Started

You should:

  • Visit the ASC website using your TigerNet log-in credentials
  • Complete the questions
  • ASC Video: If you interviewed for ASC last year, you do not need to watch the video again. New interviews must watch the video and enter a code displayed at the end of the video to confirm this.
  • Questions: Respond using numbers only

Two Ways to Get Matched

“Grab and Go”

  • Select time slots that work for you
  • Applicants indicate availability
  • Match is made official
  • Confirm preselected time with applicant

Jump right in! No email ping-pong.

Traditional

  • Accept assigned matches
  • Arrange time directly with candidate
  • Schedule at mutual convenience
  • Flexible scheduling

Maximum flexibility for both parties.

After You Have Been Matched

Accept the Interview

Applicant's name will appear in “Interviews Assigned to You”. Accept the interview by clicking the check box next to “Accept – Interview Not Yet Completed” and then “Report Responses” to submit

Reach Out

Once you receive an assignment, please contact the student within 48 hours. Candidates receive an email when you accept the interview and tend to get nervous if they don't hear from you.

No Reply?

If the student does not respond to your email, click on the applicant's name to see mobile and home telephone numbers to call. Our usual rule is two emails and one phone call (or text message) before marking an applicant as non-responsive.

Meeting the Applicant

Ambassadors

Our role as ASC interviewers is to serve as ambassador for Princeton.

Every applicant should be treated with respect and leave with a good impression of Princeton and the process.

Informal

Applicants must have the option for a virtual interview. If you are not able to honor the applicant's preference for a virtual interview, we can find a different match.

Conversations should be informal and last around 30-45 minutes.

The interview is not about their grades, their likelihood of admission or a test of their knowledge.

Princeton's low acceptance rate means many applicants will not be admitted; however, every applicant deserves a positive experience.

Communications

Your candidate report is most useful when:

  • It is substantive with specific examples and observations
  • It arrives well in advance of the report deadline. The Admission Office benefits from more time to consider your report.

Complete your report within one week of conducting the interview, while impressions are still vivid.

Areas to Discuss in Your Report

  • Your impression of the candidate's personal qualities, with supporting examples when possible
  • Is this someone you could imagine interacting positively and contributing in campus activities?
  • Unusual circumstances about the applicant's background, local area, experiences, or accomplishments
  • Were there particular limitations or special opportunities that impacted this student's experiences?
  • Your sense of the student's intellectual curiosity
  • Can they effectively discuss a favorite academic area?
  • Can they describe in detail a project or paper that was particularly interesting to them?
  • Is there a book, film or work of art that has been meaningful to them?
  • If you and the candidate did not hit it off, tell us that and explain why
